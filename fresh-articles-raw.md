The outfield is slower than Mumbai or Bengaluru; both spinners and medium-pacers extract meaningful assistance from the surface by the 12th over.

*Captain options:* Yashasvi Jaiswal is the premier fantasy asset in this fixture. His record against CSK in T20 cricket is exceptional (average: 41.3 in 9 head-to-head appearances), and Guwahati's slightly slower outfield rewards his ground-hitting game. Ruturaj Gaikwad (VC) is the CSK equivalent — composed, consistent, and capable of a match-defining knock in conditions that reward technical batters.

*Wildcard pick:* Ravindra Jadeja versus his former team is the narrative pick that also has statistical backing. His record against current CSK batters includes 11 dismissals of Ruturaj Gaikwad in 43 T20 deliveries. If you're willing to carry a bowler-allrounder as a differential captain, Jadeja's combination of wickets and lower-order runs at Guwahati represents genuine value.

*Injury note:* Sam Curran absent (replaced by Dasun Shanaka, who was registered with BCCI on March 23). Shanaka is ineligible for fantasy picks until officially confirmed in Playing XI. Matthew Short (CSK, fractured thumb) is absent from early fixtures.

The opening weekend sets fantasy league hierarchies that persist through the season. The players who score big in matches 1-3 become the default captain picks through April, compounding their ownership advantages. Back Kohli for RCB on Saturday. He has never needed a reason to start a season well at Chinnaswamy.
